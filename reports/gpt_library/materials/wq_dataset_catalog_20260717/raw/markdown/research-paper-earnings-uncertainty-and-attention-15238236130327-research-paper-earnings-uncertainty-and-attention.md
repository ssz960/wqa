# Research Paper: Earnings Uncertainty and Attention


Source: https://support.worldquantbrain.com/hc/en-us/community/posts/15238236130327-Research-Paper-Earnings-Uncertainty-and-Attention


Abstract:

I examine the relationship between earnings uncertainty and attention to subsequent earnings releases and how the relationship affects the stock price response to earnings. I use the percentage of uncertain words in 10-K or 10-Q filings as the measure of ex ante earnings uncertainty. Earnings releases of high uncertainty firms are accompanied by higher Google search volume, higher Bloomberg readership, higher abnormal trading volume, and faster analyst response suggesting that they receive higher attention. When investors have finite attention resources, they might not be fully attentive to all information. To the extent attention constraints are binding, more attention to high uncertainty firms will result in lesser attention to low uncertainty firms. Limited attention literature has shown that lower attention to earnings will lead to underreaction of stock prices which is captured by stronger post earnings announcement drift (PEAD). I find that PEAD is stronger in low uncertainty firms consistent with their stock prices underreacting more to earnings. The results are not explained by other factors affecting PEAD documented in the literature. The findings are consistent with higher attention to high uncertainty firms resulting in higher PEAD in low uncertainty firms when attention constraints are binding.
